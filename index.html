Come discover Singapore's tech community

Tech is thriving in Singapore! For one afternoon, we've gathered some of the best and most exciting names in our local tech community, all in one place. Come see what they do, hear their stories, and ask them all the questions you've been meaning to ask! Whether you're an anxious student, a fresh graduate, or simply someone with zero tech-experience just curious about what's out there - Tech Fest is for everyone!

Find out what it's like to work in tech and see the cool things you can be a part of

We've got everyone from global tech giants, to home grown companies, to up-and-coming startups. Come ask them what life in tech is like, check out awesome demos, and play with the latest tech products! Discover all the cool things people are doing and find out how you too, can be a part of that.

Hear firsthand experiences from engineers, startup founders, product designers, and more

Tan Min Liang

Razer Co-founder

Tan Min Liang

Min-Liang Tan is the co-founder, CEO and chief gamer of Razer, the world’s leading brand in entertainment devices and software, where he plays an instrumental role in shaping Razer’s success in its current years of unprecedented growth both within the company and the gaming industry. As one of the co-founders of the company, he has built the company from inception to a multi-million dollar global organization.

Min-Liang Tan is responsible for providing strategic leadership to Razer by establishing its long-range goals, strategies, plans and policies as the company expands into next generation user interfaces developed specifically for gaming. He is also responsible for the design and development of all the iconic products that the brand is known for.

Being a gamer himself, Min-Liang Tan was recently elected to the board of the PC Gaming Alliance where he brings valuable insight and knowledge to the committee. He has built a cult following around the Razer brand in the past seven years as he continues to lead the company to be the world’s greatest gaming company with his uncanny ability of harnessing existing technologies. Min-Liang Tan has been named one of "The 25 Most Creative People in Tech" by the Business Insider together with Jonathan Ive and Gabe Newell. He has also been ranked one of the top 40 most powerful people in gaming by Kotaku in their "The Kotaku Power 40" list.

Min-Liang Tan was born in Singapore and resides in San Francisco where he oversees the company’s global operations spanning offices in the USA, Germany, Singapore, China, Korea and Taiwan. He graduated from the National University of Singapore Law School and was a lawyer before he created Razer with Robert Krakoff.

Sergio Salvador

Google - Global Head Games Partnerships

Sergio Salvador

Sergio has accumulated more than 15 years of diverse experience in General Management, Marketing, and Strategic Business Development across different industries and countries. He has launched and managed startups, and held various roles in multinational companies.

During his time with Electronic Arts and Nokia, Sergio focused on building and bringing to market software, applications and digital services in the videogames, mobile and digital media spaces. Sergio's focus in the past few years has been on building strong partnerships and alliances for Yahoo! first and then for Google across Southeast Asia, through a variety of initiatives with global and local players, to positively influence the growth of the digital industry across the region. More recently, Sergio took on the challenge of leading a new team focused on partnerships with games companies to bring about the next phase in the development of the games market.

Sergio values rolling up his sleeves and working closely with internal and external stakeholders to drive business results. He is an avid student and practitioner of digital and social media marketing.

Sergio is also a strong advocate of paying back to the community, which he does through his work with higher education institutions, using his extensive professional experience to educate the next generation of digital media, games, and technology professionals. In his personal life, Sergio loves to spend time with his kids, and practice various sports including tennis, running, and yoga.

Bunnie Huang

Kosagi - Co-Founder

Bunnie Huang

Bunnie loves hardware. He loves to make it, and to break it; he loves the smell of it. His passion for hardware began in elementary school, and since then he has garnered a PhD at MIT in EE, and has designed nanophotonic silicon chips, wireless radios, consumer electronics, robotic submarines, among other things. He was involved in some of the earliest stages of hardware reverse engineering on the Xbox, and his experiences are summarized in his book, "Hacking the Xbox: An Introduction to Reverse Engineering". bunnie believes hardware is delightful in part because there are no secrets in hardware; you just need a better microscope. Likewise, he is a proponent of open source hardware, and is an active contributor to the ecosystem. At chumby, he designed several open source hardware platforms, some of which had found its way to the shelves of retailers around the world. bunnie is also an educator; he serves as a Research Affiliate for the MIT Media Lab, technical advisor for several hardware startups and MAKE magazine, and shares his experiences manufacturing hardware in China through his blog.

In 2012 he received an Electronic Frontier Foundation Pioneer Award for his work in hardware hacking, open source and activism. He currently lives in Singapore.

Sean Cross

Kosagi - Co-Founder

Sean Cross

Xobs is a firmware hacker, and enjoys figuring out how hardware and software interact, learning new things, and canoeing. Occasionally he finds himself thinking C is too high-level a language.

Xobs has always taken things apart to figure out how they work. From childhood toys to vacuum cleaners, nothing was safe from disassembly Occasionally he tries to put things back together again, which is always a test of comprehension.

He grew up and went to school in San Diego, California. At university, to an administrative quirk, the school of computing was unavailable, so he earned a degree in cognitive science instead, because people are like squishy computers, right?

After university, he got a job working at chumby Industries, where he met Bunnie. There, he got his first shot at creating low level device firmware, and figuring out how to create computers from scratch. He did much the firmware for the chumby One.

Chumby moved him to Singapore, where he did system firmware for the NeTV. After the end of chumby, he stayed in Singapore, and continues to do system design and integration.

Justin Halim

Facebook - Head of Agencies, Southeast Asia

Justin Halim

Justin Halim is Head of Agencies, Southeast Asia, based in Singapore. In this capacity Justin leads a team focused on being indispensable partners to the industry’s largest Holding Companies and its agencies, revolutionizing the way both agencies and Facebook grow together. Prior to this Justin was Agency Development Lead, focused specifically on partnering with WPP and its agencies in providing the education, insight, systems and tools required to help enable its clients to be successful on Facebook.

Justin is a long-time veteran of the WPP Group, to which he credits many of his career achievements to date. Having cultivated a curiosity around the advertising and media industry at the Wunderman/Y&R Group in Sydney, Justin commenced in the industry working on Telstra and developing a successful agency new business track record with several key Australian Government project wins. International ambitions and a more focused passion for digital and direct took Justin to Wunderman UK, working across brands such as Ford, Land Rover, and finally a founding member of Wunderman's Global Nokia business. The agency saw the opportunity to leverage this Global experience in Asia, taking Justin to Wunderman Singapore to lead the 13-strong Nokia SEAP business, followed by responsibility for leading and building out a centralized digital marketing and production team for Microsoft APAC – a cross-functional team of some 40 team members residing in both Singapore and Manila, delivering across 9 Microsoft APAC markets. Finally, Justin was instrumental in the new business space leading digital as part of WPP's Team P&G across ASEAN.

Most recently Justin has joined from LinkedIn, leading strategy development and ideation to drive large marketing solutions in collaboration with APAC sales teams in Hong Kong, Singapore, and Australia-New Zealand.

Spanning a 13-year career digital has become not only a profession, but also passion and past-time – Justin is deeply passionate about linking innovation, digital marketing and technology with strategic and creative thinking to drive business success.

Oliver Tan

Visenze - Founder

Oliver Tan

Before co-founding ViSenze, Oliver held senior roles at PT Media Nusantara Citra (MNC - Indonesia’s largest multimedia company) and at MediaCorp where he led in strategy and business development, partnerships, web analytics and ad ops for Interactive Media. In 2009, he was instrumental in establishing XinMSN, one of Singapore’s largest entertainment portals. Before that, he spent 5 eventful years in an award-winning web security startup (until its successful buyout) where he rolled his sleeves and led in all aspects of its corporate, business,market development as well as group ops management across 3 countries. At e-Cop, he helped the then young company become EBITDA positive within 3 years of operations and nailed a series of achievements. Oliver started his early years in venture capital in IndoChina before progressing to executing M&A projects for Pacific Internet (now PacNet), Singapore Telecom(SingTel) and MediaCorp in his later years, collectively completing more than a dozen multi-million dollar deals in between.

Smitten by so much fun from his prior 5 years experience in a startup, Oliver decided to leave a successful corporate career of over 15 years in 2013 to go back to his first true love - tech startups !! – by becoming one himself together. As the founding CEO of ViSenze, a pioneer startup in intelligent visual recognition and search - he rolls up his shelve again to helm ViSenze, with goal of becoming a global leader in visual tech space. In 2013, he led the company to partner Rakuten to launch the region’s first ever visual-search driven ecommerce site in Taiwan. Subsequently in Feb 2014, he led ViSenze to close its first major funding of US$3.5 million (Series A) from marque investors, including Rakuten. A passionate start-up believer, Oliver is driven by making a difference in things he does, and so shares his experience willingly, having crossed from corporate to startup twice.

Oliver earned his bachelor’s degree with honours in economics and MBA with distinction from Royal Holloway, University of London. In his free time, he trains for marathons and enjoys cycling.
